# Marth's Enchanting Overhaul (MEO)

A full replacement for Skyrim SE enchanting that turns enchantments into
**gems**: socketed into your own equipment from a pouch menu anywhere, and
**leveled through use**. Weapons, armor, and jewelry gain sockets; staves,
bound weapons, and scripted artifacts are detected and left alone.

Built entirely from Linux with no Creation Kit and no xEdit: the ESP is
generated byte-by-byte from Python, the gem catalog is parsed straight out of
the game masters, and the native SKSE plugin (CommonLibSSE-NG) builds on
GitHub Actions.

> **Status: 1.0 release candidate (v0.49.4).** Feature-complete and validated
> in-game: the native socket core, all 54 gems (51 normal + 3 support), loot
> conversion, the installer with per-load-order calibration, the perk tree,
> menu skins, and controller support all ship and work. Remaining before
> 1.0.0: final documentation/portability audit and release engineering.

## The idea

- **Enchantments become gems.** Every generic enchantment collapses into a
  gem *family* (the catalog is generated from ~740 ENCH records in the game
  masters, deduplicated by effect). Weapon gems are on-hit effects; armor
  gems are constant effects. 51 normal families ship, each at levels I–V,
  plus 3 support gems (below) at tiers I–III.
- **Socket anywhere, lose nothing.** The Gem Pouch power opens the socket
  menu with no crafting station. Socketing and unsocketing your own gems is
  always free, lossless, and reversible — an unsocketed gem returns as *the
  same gem*, carrying its own level and banked XP. Loose gems live in a
  hidden pouch container, not your inventory, so they never clutter it and
  can never be sold by accident (gems have no gold value, deliberately).
- **Gems level I → V through your kills**, then a mastered gem *births* a
  fresh level-I copy of itself — the only way to replicate one. Enchanting
  stations get a second life: feed filled soul gems to a socketed gem for XP,
  or destroy a gem to reclaim part of its essence as a soul gem.
- **Enchanted loot converts.** Generic enchanted loot — drops, chests,
  vendors, world items, all vanilla + Creation Club sources — converts on
  acquisition into the plain item with the matching gem socketed and active
  (~87% of generic enchanted gear on the development load order). Uniques,
  artifacts, and distinctive named sets keep their enchantments and their
  sockets are sealed.
- **Sockets scale with skill.** Gloves are dual-socket by default; perks add
  a second linked socket to chest armor and weapons. A linked pair means both
  gems ride one item — and both earn XP from every kill.
- **Support gems** (Focus, Conduit, Echo) do nothing alone but transform the
  normal gem they share a dual-socket item with — magnitude boosts,
  cross-domain adaptation, AoE procs, follower sharing.
- **Gems never drain.** No charge, no recharging. The trade is that power
  comes from the gem's level, which you earn.
- **The item is the database.** Socket state lives on each item instance and
  in the SKSE co-save — no Papyrus save bloat, no side-table to desync.

## Feature summary (all shipped, in-game confirmed)

- 51 gem families (15 weapon + 36 armor), each level I–V with birthing at V.
- One combined instance enchantment per item; multi-socket with linked pairs.
- The hidden Gem Pouch: socket/unsocket/swap anywhere via a native in-game
  menu (ImGui, drawn inside the game's own renderer), with one-click gem swap
  for filled sockets.
- Loot conversion of pre-enchanted gear at spawn/acquire — all acquisition
  paths (corpses, chests, vendors, world refs, pickups) plus Creation Club
  content; uniques and artifacts preserved.
- Per-load-order calibration: the installer derives gem magnitudes, recipe
  riders, and the conversion table from *your* winning records — nothing is
  hardcoded to the dev machine's list.
- Rank ladders: gem levels climb your list's own enchantment rank ladder,
  and the ladder is explained in-game on the gem itself.
- The MEO perk tree, installed into your load order's enchanting tree:
  Gem Attunement 1–5, Gem Cutter, Soul Feeder, the elemental affinities
  (Pyrestone / Froststone / Stormstone, +25% each), Facet Insight (+25%
  skill/attribute armor gems), Twinned Fitting, and Master Jeweler.
- Support gems: Focus, Conduit, Echo (see "How to play").
- Enchanting stations: soul feeding, gem destruction, and (by default) full
  station takeover — the gem menu replaces the vanilla enchanting menu.
- Temper socketed gear without Arcane Blacksmith (MCM toggle, default on).
- 2-of-a-kind stacking cap: at most two gems of the same effect count across
  all worn gear.
- Circlets are socketable (their own biped slot, distinct from helmets).
- 4 menu skins (Ebony & Brass, Dwemer Parchment, Soul Cairn, Quicksilver),
  switchable live from the MCM.
- Full controller support in the gem menu (stick + d-pad navigation).

## Installing

Each release zip is a complete MO2 mod — install it like any other. Then one
post-install step:

1. Enable the mod (left pane) and `MEO.esp` (right pane).
2. Open the mod's folder (right-click → *Open in Explorer*) and run
   **`MEO.Installer.exe`**. It finds your MO2 instance on its own (vanilla /
   non-MO2 setups are also supported), reads your *actual* load order
   (nothing is hardcoded or assumed), and:
   - generates `MEO - Patch.esp`: your enchanting perk tree with its crafting
     perks replaced by MEO's gem perks. Any other perk it finds in the tree
     (staff/wand utility perks and the like) it shows you — description,
     effects, abilities — and asks whether to keep, one `[Y/n]` per perk.
     Answers persist in `MEO - Patch.choices.json`; edit or delete it and
     re-run to change your mind.
   - writes `meo_calibration.json`: per-list gem magnitudes, recipe riders,
     and the enchanted-loot conversion table, all derived from your winning
     records. Re-run the installer after modlist updates.
3. Enable `MEO - Patch.esp` **near the end of your load order** (after
   anything that edits the enchanting perk tree). `MEO.esp` itself declares a
   single master (Skyrim.esm) and can sit anywhere.

The installer is a .NET CLI tool (Mutagen), built untrimmed on public GitHub
CI — it never launches or touches the game, only reads your load order files
and writes its outputs. It runs natively on Windows *and* Linux (MO2-under-
Proton setups work unchanged).

## How to play

There is no tutorial quest and no in-game primer — this section is the
onboarding.

1. **Open the Gem Pouch** — a lesser power granted on first load (the
   shout/power key also closes the menu). Pick an item, pick a gem, socket
   it. Free, anywhere, reversible.
2. **Kill things.** Every kill feeds Gem XP to every socketed gem in your
   worn gear (and followers' kills feed *their* worn gems). Gems climb
   I → V; magnitude scales with level. Past level V a gem is Mastered and
   births a fresh level-I copy — the only replication there is.
3. **Loot converts itself.** Generic enchanted drops arrive as plain gear
   with the matching gem already socketed — unsocket it for free and the gem
   is yours. Uniques and artifacts stay enchanted and sealed.
4. **Enchanting tables** now open the gem menu (MCM-toggleable). There you
   can feed filled soul gems to a socketed gem for instant XP
   (petty/lesser/common/greater/grand = 1 / 2.5 / 5 / 12 / 40 Gem XP;
   the Soul Feeder perk doubles it) or destroy a gem to reclaim a tenth of
   its banked XP as a soul gem.
5. **Dual sockets and support gems.** Gloves hold two linked gems from the
   start; perks unlock second linked sockets on chest and main-hand weapon.
   A support gem linked to a normal gem transforms it:
   - **Focus** — a linked elemental gem (Fire/Frost/Shock/Chaos) gains
     +20/35/50% magnitude by tier.
   - **Conduit** — a cross-domain adapter: an off-domain gem works through
     its item-domain sibling (e.g. an armor gem's theme expressed as the
     weapon-domain equivalent) at a 50/75/100% transfer ratio by tier.
   - **Echo** — in a weapon: the linked on-hit effect gains an area,
     hitting hostile/in-combat actors around the target; in armor: the
     linked gem's effect is shared with your current followers.
   Support gems level I–III (XP only while linked; they never birth). They
   are rare: a low-chance drop from boss/dragon kills at player level 15+,
   plus one guaranteed hand-placed copy each — Focus in Avanchnzel, Conduit
   in the Midden's Atronach Forge offering box, Echo in Ustengrav Depths.
6. **Perks.** After the installer runs, your enchanting perk tree grants the
   MEO perks: Gem Attunement (magnitude, 5 ranks), Gem Cutter (+50% Gem XP),
   Soul Feeder (×2 feeding), the three elemental affinities and Facet
   Insight (+25% to their gem classes), Twinned Fitting and Master Jeweler
   (second linked sockets). Your Enchanting *skill* still matters: Gem XP
   gain scales with it, and Fortify Enchanting potions/gear scale it further.

## MCM options

Everything gameplay-tunable is an MCM setting paired with a live-read INI
(`MCM/Settings/MEO.ini`), applied on menu close — no restart:

- **Loot & Spawns**: gem drop chance per kill, world-weapon socket chance,
  level-II spawn chance, enemy socketed-gear chance, vendor gem chance.
- **XP & Balance**: Gem XP rate, boss/dragon XP multiplier, gem power scale,
  enchanting skill XP from souls.
- **UI**: Gem XP notifications, "gem menu replaces enchanting table" toggle,
  temper-without-Arcane-Blacksmith toggle, gem menu style (4 skins).
- **Debug**: "Grant all MEO perks (testing)" (`bDebugAllPerks`) — forces
  every MEO perk on for testing without spending points; toggling off
  reverts to what you actually hold.

A few extra tunables are INI-only (same file): support-gem drop chance,
support-gem minimum player level, and the dev Gem-XP-per-kill base.

## Known issues

- **Some floor items can vanish after save → main menu → load.** A suspected
  engine save-culling issue, external to MEO; socketed items *in inventory*
  persist correctly across save/load.
- **Worn gem effects re-arm shortly after loading a save.** The engine does
  not re-register instance enchantments on its own at load; MEO re-applies
  them automatically within a few seconds of the loading screen closing. If
  a weapon ever seems inert right after a load, wait a moment or re-equip.
- **World weapons on racks/displays and in NPC hands are not born
  pre-socketed** — the world pre-socket roll applies to loose world
  references only. (Enchanted-loot *conversion* is separate and does cover
  containers, corpses, vendors, and pickups.)
- **A small tail of enchanted loot does not convert** (~13% on the dev
  list): duration-anchored recipes not yet expressible as gem riders (e.g.
  some paralysis/soul-trap companions) and new-family effects with no gem
  coverage. These keep spawning as regular enchanted items. Uniques and
  artifacts are *intentionally* untouched.
- MEO replaces enchanting for weapons and armor. Staves, wands, bound
  weapons, and scripted artifacts keep vanilla behavior end to end.

If something misbehaves, grab `SKSE/MEO.log` (in your `My Games/.../SKSE/`
folder) — the first line names the exact build, and every system logs what it
does.

## Architecture

- **`MEO.esp`** — generated by `MEO_GenerateESP.py` (never hand-edited):
  gem MISC items, magic-effect markers, the pouch power + hidden pouch
  container, perk records, MCM quest. FormIDs are frozen once shipped; the
  generator is freeze-aware.
- **`MEO.dll`** — CommonLibSSE-NG SKSE plugin, built on GitHub Actions
  (windows-latest, MSVC): the socket engine, created-enchantment stamping,
  kill-XP and leveling, loot conversion, support-gem linking, the in-game
  ImGui gem menu, perk effects, and versioned co-save serialization.
  Address-Library-based.
- **MCM** — MCM Helper config paired with an INI; every gameplay tunable is
  live-editable in game.
- **`MEO.Installer.exe`** — C# (Mutagen) post-install patcher, CI-built
  (win-x64, self-contained): resolves the load order without the VFS,
  derives calibration + conversion from the winning records, and writes
  `MEO - Patch.esp` with interactive perk curation.
- Papyrus is compile-time only (one empty MCM stub), compiled under Proton
  wine from Linux.

## FAQ

**Doesn't socketing anywhere, for free, make enchanting trivial?**
Socketing is free; *power* isn't. A fresh gem starts at level I and only
levels through kills made with it equipped — the investment moves from the
crafting table into actual use. Your Enchanting skill still matters, more
directly than in vanilla: it scales every gem's XP gain, and the perk layer
adds magnitude, XP, and second-socket unlocks on top.

**Isn't a high-level gem overpowered?**
It's earned. Reaching level V takes a long ladder of kill XP, and a mastered
gem's one reward beyond its own power is birthing a single level-I copy.
Gem drops follow a tier rarity curve (the strongest families are the
rarest), the stacking cap stops single-stat pile-ups, and everything sits
behind MCM sliders if your list needs different pacing.

**Why don't gems ever drain?**
Deliberately. Charge maintenance is busywork that punishes using your best
gear; MEO removes it and gates power behind gem levels instead. Soul gems
keep a role — a better one: feed them to a socketed gem at any enchanting
station for permanent Gem XP rather than a temporary refill.

**What happens to enchanted loot and artifacts?**
Generic enchanted loot converts into socketed gear (the gem is the loot);
uniques, artifacts, and named sets keep their enchantments, sealed. Items
with `MagicDisallowEnchanting`, staves, bound weapons, and scripted
artifacts are detected and excluded.

**Will it work with my load order?**
`MEO.esp` declares a single master (Skyrim.esm) and references no external
records; the DLL is Address-Library-based, and the installer calibrates the
numbers to *your* list's winning records. Socket state lives in a versioned
SKSE co-save that migrates itself forward across updates.
